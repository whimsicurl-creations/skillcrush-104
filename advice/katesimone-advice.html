Hi my name is Kate and I have some advice for you!

It's all about problem solving!

You might run into all kinds of weird problems when you code. From floats that won't float to colors in the wrong spot to divs that aren't closed: there are one million tiny things that can go wrong.

But that's okay! Because every mistake that you fix is a thing you learned and one step closer to being a great coder.

Use the community!

Some days when I just didn't get something, heading to the Slack channels made all the difference. I often didn't even need to reach out to ask a question because often someone else had asked it already. AND, even more importantly, seeing other people talk about their challenges and wins made me feel like I wasn't alone.

Learning something new is hard and having other people to talk to makes all the difference.

Just keep swimming!

I missed a few days. Womp womp. I was annoyed with myself and then felt like I forgot all the stuff I learned... But then I had a little talk with myself and said: Kate, remember how good it felt when the code worked? Let's do it again.

Life is really busy and sometimes I just don't have time to work on this thing. But all those tiny steps on the days when I do my classes are adding up to one awesome coder!
